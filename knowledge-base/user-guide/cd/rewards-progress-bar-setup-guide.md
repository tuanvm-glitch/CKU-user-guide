# Rewards Progress Bar - Setup Guide

### What This Does (and Why You'll Want It)

The **Rewards Progress Bar** shows customers how close they are to unlocking rewards right inside the cart. As they add products, the bar fills up and messages update in real time, nudging them to add just a bit more.

To find it, go to **Cart Editor** → **Rewards Progress Bar**.

**Why this matters for your store:**

* Nudges customers to add one more item
* Boosts average order value with clear rewards
* Makes your cart feel fun and goal-based
* Reduces guesswork about free shipping or perks
* Helps you promote multiple rewards at once

### Who Can Use This Feature?

This feature is available for all users.

***

### What You’ll Need

Before you jump into setup, it helps to plan a few things:

* Decide if you want rewards based on **Total cart value** or **Product quantity**.
* Choose 2–4 rewards you want to promote (for example: free shipping, small discount, free gift).
* Write short, friendly messages for each step, like “Add one more item for free shipping.”
* Think about a fun final message for **Title upon achieving all tiers**, such as “You unlocked all rewards!”

Expect setup to take about **10–15 minutes** once you’ve decided your thresholds and messages.

***

### Setting This Up

You only need a few steps to get a great-looking **Rewards Progress Bar** live.

1. **Open the Rewards Progress Bar settings**\
   Go to **Cart Editor** → **Rewards Progress Bar**. You’ll see options for how the bar behaves, what it says, and how it looks.
2.  **Choose your Tiered reward type**

    At the top, in **Tiered reward type**, pick how progress should be calculated:

    * **Total cart value** – rewards based on how much customers spend.
    * **Product quantity** – rewards based on how many items are in the cart.

    Choose the one that best fits your store’s pricing and goals.
3. **Add your reward tiers**
   * Click **Add tier** to create your first reward step.
   * For each tier, fill in:
     * **Reward {N}** – the short reward title customers see when it’s unlocked (for example, “Free Shipping” or “Free Gift”).
     * **Message before achieving tier {N}** – the teaser message shown when the tier is not yet reached (for example, “Spend $20 more for free shipping”).
   * Add 2–4 tiers to build a simple reward ladder.
4. **Write your final success message**
   * In **Title upon achieving all tiers**, write the headline that appears when customers unlock every tier.
   * Keep it fun and celebratory, like:
     * “You did it! All rewards unlocked.”
     * “Wow! You grabbed every perk in this order.”
5.  **Customize the look and feel**

    Use the color settings to match your brand and make progress crystal clear:

    * **Title color** – for the main heading text.
    * **Reached text color** and **Unreached text color** – to visually separate unlocked vs locked rewards.
    * **Reached icon color** and **Unreached icon color** – for the icons next to each tier.
    * **Reached bar background** and **Unreached bar background** – for the filled and unfilled parts of the progress bar.

    Aim for strong contrast so customers can instantly tell what’s unlocked.
6.  **Test the experience**

    * Open your storefront and add items to the cart.
    * Watch the bar as you increase cart value or item quantity:
      * Do messages change at the right moments?
      * Are the colors clear and readable?
      * Does the final message feel exciting?

    Adjust thresholds, messages, or colors until it feels natural and motivating.

{% hint style="success" %}
**Pro tip:** Pretend you’re a new customer. Start with a small cart, then slowly add more. If the bar makes _you_ want to add “just one more,” you’ve nailed it.
{% endhint %}

### Essential Settings

Here are the core settings you’ll use most often.

**Tiered reward type**

* **What it controls:** Whether progress is based on **Total cart value** or **Product quantity**.
* **Recommended value:** Start with **Total cart value** if your prices vary a lot.
* **Example:** “Spend $50 for free shipping, $100 for a free gift.”

**Reward {N}**

* **What it controls:** The reward title shown when a tier is reached.
* **Recommended value:** Short and clear, like “Free Shipping” or “10% Off.”
* **Example:** Tier 1: “Free Shipping,” Tier 2: “Free Tote Bag.”

**Message before achieving tier {N}**

* **What it controls:** The teaser message shown while the reward is still locked.
* **Recommended value:** Use a friendly nudge plus a clear goal.
* **Example:** “Spend $15 more to unlock free shipping.”

**Title upon achieving all tiers**

* **What it controls:** The main headline when all rewards are unlocked.
* **Recommended value:** Make it celebratory and specific.
* **Example:** “You unlocked free shipping, $10 off, and a free gift!”

**Reached bar background / Unreached bar background**

* **What they control:** The colors of the filled and unfilled sections of the progress bar.
* **Recommended value:** Use your brand color for “reached” and a light neutral for “unreached.”
* **Example:** Reached = bright green, Unreached = light gray.

### Frequently Asked Questions

<details>

<summary>How many reward tiers should I create?</summary>

Most stores see the best results with **2–4 tiers**.

* 1 tier is simple but less motivating.
* 2–3 tiers create a “ladder” effect where customers feel pulled to the next step.
* More than 4 tiers can feel confusing or overwhelming.

Start with 2 or 3, then adjust based on how your customers respond.

</details>

<details>

<summary>Should I use Total cart value or Product quantity?</summary>

Use **Total cart value** if your products have different prices or you care most about Average Order Value. Use **Product quantity** if your products are similarly priced or you want customers to add more units (for example, T-shirts, socks, or beauty items). You can experiment and switch later if you like.

</details>

<details>

<summary>What kind of messages work best for tiers?</summary>

Short, specific, and friendly messages work best. Mention both the action and the reward. For example:

* “Spend $15 more to unlock free shipping.”
* “Add 1 more item for 10% off your order.”\
  Avoid vague lines like “Almost there!” without telling them what “there” is.

</details>

<details>

<summary>Will customers see the progress bar on every cart?</summary>

Yes, as long as the **Rewards Progress Bar** is enabled in your **Cart Editor** setup, customers see it whenever they open the cart and have items inside. You control the thresholds and messages, and the bar updates automatically based on their cart.

</details>
